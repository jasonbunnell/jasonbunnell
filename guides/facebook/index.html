Summary

This is a quick guide that covers basic Facebook marketing. I will cover creating a facebook page (like the one linked to in this paragraph), creating content for your page, and running paid campaigns for this page. Also, I will briefly cover how to theoretically get free traffic and how to use campaigns to build an audience and get traffic.

Basic Navigation

There are three main navigation menus. The Main Menu is the top horizontal menu at the top of every page on Facebook. The background is blue and includes the seach box to the right of the Facebook icon. Aside from the search icon, you can see the name you are currently logged in as, Home (which takes you back to the main page), and 4 icons and a dropdown that are all in a darker shade of blue.

Facebook Search

You can search just like you would on a search engine, but you can refine searches by looking within several categories. The search categories are Posts, People, Photos, Videos, Pages, Places, Groups, Apps, Events, and Links. So if you do a search for Jason Bunnell, it will likely default to showing you results in the People category. You can tell because that section will say "People". If you click "See All", you will see all the results of people with that name or people with a similar name. If you wanted to see photos of Jason Bunnell, you can click the Photos sub-menu option. A vague search like Dallas hotels will show Places first and then Pages. The Places section will say Hotels and not Places and the results will be addresses. The Places section will also have a map below the title section. Places will be replaced by a category of place like Restaurants, Hotels, Grocery Stores or any number of retail shop categories.

Facebook will highlight words in the results in light blue which indicate why it served those results specifically. As pages or people submit posts that contain certain keywords, they will likely rise in ranking for that keyword. Additionally, Facebook will look at a number of metrics like Likes, Comments, and Shares. Collectively Likes, Comments, and Shares are called engagement. You can go the the Metrics section for more information, but basically Facebook uses engagement metrics to determine how to rank search results and how prominently to show posts from People or a Page.

Drop Down Menu

The Drop Down Menu icon looks like a triangle pointing down and is located on the far right of the Main Menu. This menu lets you create and manage Pages, Groups, and Ads. It will also list your pages. These pages include any pages that you have access to. The page owner can add any number of people and grant various functionality.

Metrics

Conversion - the ratio of goal conversions to clicks where goal conversion may be a sale or some other desired action taken by the user.

Click Through Rate (CTR) - ratio of impressions to clicks. If an ad has 1,000 impressions and 10 clicks it is said to have a CTR of 1%.

Engagement - the sum of likes, shares, and comments.

Impressions - the total page views recieved by a post, a page, or a marketing campaign.
